Initiatives

The Hope Trust trading as Ithemba Trust was established to provide funds for educational and charitable institutions of a public character in the Republic of South Africa.

 

The Ithemba Trust is a charitable trust primarily committed to the funding of projects that support therapeutic and psychosocial interventions in the development of differently abled children.

 

The Ithemba Trust’s initial focus was assisting in alleviating problems such as lack of knowledge, training, skills or diagnostic ability to effectively deal with the treatment of children with disabilities in disadvantaged areas of Gauteng, Kwazulu Natal and the Western and Eastern Cape.

 

The Trust funded skilled professionals to initiate socially empowering, multidisciplinary, hands-on training programs for care workers within local communities involving the physical and intellectual development of each child.

 

The Ithemba Trust has over the years extended its focus to include other charitable and educational institutions and support them with the funding of learning programmes, capacity building of staff and carers, the supply of therapeutic equipment and offering psychosocial interventions such as camp experiences with intentional learning and play for children with disabilities and those with serious health and life challenges.

The Just Footprints Foundation was founded in February 2008 and strives to create a special journey of life-changing experiences,

for each child, which will leave an imprint on their hearts with hope for the future.

 

They create opportunities for children and their families or guardians to reach beyond their serious illness and challenges and discover joy, confidence and a new world of possibilities, always free of charge, in a safe, nurturing, fun camp environment backed by appropriate medical support.

The Trust has given young unemployed youth the opportunity to participate in various volunteer programmes that the Trust supports at schools and organisations eg assisting with sports training with special needs children for the Special Olympics; support at annual sports days and events such as Walk the Talk by transporting of wheelchairs/walkers etc and being the children’s  buddies which allows the children to participate in the activities and feel part of the events; volunteer at camp events and camp outreach and hospital programmes as facilitators, sports coaches and activity leaders to support, care for and mentor the campers. The camp volunteers participate in training and upskilling workshops, team building and leadership opportunities. The staff and carers at beneficiary organisations who attend camp are also put through a training programme before coming to camp.
